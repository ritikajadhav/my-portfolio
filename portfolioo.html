Seculinx – Interactive Website for a Home Automation Startup


Internship Project | May–July 2024


As part of my internship at a home automation startup, I designed and developed an introductory website for Seculinx.

> The company wanted a sleek, modern aesthetic with a dark edge to reflect the advanced nature of their smart home solutions.

> I incorporated luminous design elements to mimic the behavior of light, aligning with their core feature of controlling home lighting.


> To elevate user engagement, I created an interactive 3D model in Blender, which is featured on the homepage.

The final website delivers a visually immersive and intuitive experience that keeps users engaged while presenting the brand in a compelling, futuristic style.

FFCS ComboGen - website designed to simplify the FFCS process

As part of CodeChef Club

FFCS ComboGen is a playful yet practical tool designed to simplify the process of generating class combinations for VIT's FFCS system.

>Built with a strong focus on user experience, the interface mimics the feel of building blocks—using vibrant, fun colors to make planning timetables feel less like a chore and more like a game.


> The design prioritizes clarity and flow: users can easily add subjects and teachers at the top, and instantly see a clean, visually organized timetable below. Every step is intuitive, reducing friction and making it accessible even for first-time users.

This project reflects my interest in combining functionality with thoughtful, user-centered design.

TLDReel

Designed and built in 48 hours during a hackathon

TLDReel is an AI-driven educational platform that converts long-form learning material into short, scrollable reels—ideal for revision and fast-paced content consumption.


Every design decision was made with clarity and inclusivity in mind. The interface is minimal, distraction-free, and accessible to users of all ages—from school students to adult learners. Despite the high-pressure 48-hour window, I ensured the experience felt intuitive, modern, and smooth across devices.


I also created all visual assets and clipart from scratch to maintain a consistent and original design language throughout the platform, giving it a unique identity that stands out in the ed-tech space.

UX Case Study: Local Event Ticketing Platform

Goal: Make discovering and booking local events easy, fast, and inclusive.


Designed a community event ticketing platform with a focus on clear navigation, smooth payment flow, and accessibility for all ages. Through journey mapping, user personas (including a student, a retiree, and a sports fan), and competitor analysis, I identified pain points like overwhelming listings, unclear pricing, and clunky checkout processes.


My focus: Inclusive design, simplified user flows, and a trust-building interface.

Role: UX Research · Journey Mapping · Strategy

About Me

I'm a UI/UX designer who believes that design should adapt to the story it's telling. Whether it's minimalist, futuristic, or something more experimental, I tailor my style to fit the concept perfectly. I love crafting immersive and interactive experiences, often using tools like Blender and more to bring a creative edge to my work.


While I enjoy playing with color, layout, and creative elements, I also pay close attention to user experience. Whether I'm working under tight deadlines or experimenting with playful visuals, I always design with the user in mind.
